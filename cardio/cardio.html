CARDIOSCAPE

ABOUT SPLASH SCREEN

CardioScape, a division of Microscape based at the Michelson Center for Convergent Biosciences at USC, hired me to design and implement an interactive splash screen for their medical visualization application. Their platform digitizes heart specimens into 3D models, enabling medical professionals to explore and understand congenital heart defects through desktop and VR interfaces.

I was brought on specifically to design the splash screen, focusing on interaction design (IxD) and creating an engaging entry point that works seamlessly across both desktop and immersive VR environments. My goal was to build a first impression that reflected CardioScape's professional, collaborative, and high-tech aesthetic while enhancing user engagement during loading sequences.

The final design centers on a distinctive cubic 3D heart model, which I prototyped in Blender before integrating into their Unity application. For web browsers, I also coded the heart animation in p5.js to ensure consistency across platforms. The splash screen features a rotating heart that transitions from white to red and pulses with a heartbeat animation, with VR functionality allowing users to move around and examine the heart in immersive space.

Key objectives included:

  • Designing an interactive splash screen that engages users while the application initializes
  • Ensuring compatibility across Unity and web environments
  • Supporting desktop mouse interaction and VR spatial exploration
  • Creating a 3D animated heart with intuitive, meaningful interactions
  • Designing flexible animations to accommodate variable loading durations (5–30 seconds)
  • Aligning visuals and interactions with CardioScape's medical and research-focused brand

This project combined 3D modeling, animation, and interaction design for VR/AR to create an impactful and seamless user experience from the moment the app launches.

For this project, I began by defining key requirements based on a vision call with the co-founders, then quickly sketched multiple concepts to explore different directions for the splash screen's design and interaction.

From these, I selected the strongest concept — a voxel-style "Minecraft" heart — and built an initial prototype in Blender, creating the 3D model, applying textures, and developing a heartbeat animation to reflect human rhythm. To ensure cross-platform performance, I recreated the animation in p5.js for web testing and validated seamless integration into Unity, the app's development platform. I also modeled the other proposed concepts in Blender to provide the team with multiple functional options for review.

After gathering feedback, I iterated on the design within Unity, using C# scripting to build smoother, more complex animations and enhance interactivity and spatial control. Finally, I worked closely with Cardioscape's software development team to integrate the chosen animation into the application, adapting my code to align with their existing architecture for reliable performance.
