OTHER PROJECTS

SLUG

ABOUT PRODUCTS UI/UX MOTION

In 2020, amid widespread hygiene product shortages, a friend and I wondered — in our hyper-designed world, where every product competes for attention, why had bar soap been left behind? While most everyday objects had been reimagined as statement pieces, soap remained surprisingly stagnant — creative in scent and color, but stuck in its traditional shape.

A couple of years later, as I began teaching myself design software (Adobe Suite, Blender) more seriously, that question came back. I wanted to build something tangible that tied together everything I was learning: product design, branding, 3D modeling, motion design, web development. This was the genesis of Slug.

Over the course of this project, I've designed and produced six distinct soap bars, each with different functional considerations. Beyond the physical products, I also developed a cohesive visual system that bridges digital and craft aesthetics, and an e-commerce platform. I've sold over 500 bars through select San Francisco retailers and direct-to-consumer channels.

The Slug 2024–2025 collection consists of six distinct forms, each addressing specific design challenges within the constraints of a functional hygiene product. Working with glycerin-based materials allowed me to explore transparency, color, and sculptural possibilities while maintaining the core requirements of effective cleansing and durability.

MYSTERY SOAP

Mystery Soap marked the beginning of Slug Soap's design exploration. Inspired by insect lollipops and resin paperweights, these transparent bars contain objects suspended within them — transforming ordinary items into art. Each bar features raised 'slug' typography in custom block letters, adding brand recognition and tactile feedback. As the soap wears down, the interplay between smooth surfaces and dimensional text creates evolving grip zones that change throughout the product's lifecycle.

CHEESE SOAP

Cheese Soap emerged from user research identifying grip as the primary failure point of traditional soap. Its wedge form references Swiss cheese, with characteristic holes serving as intuitive grip points and visual markers. I spent considerable time determining hole placement to maintain structural integrity while maximizing lathering surface area. The triangular profile naturally fits palm contours, while flat faces provide stable storage surfaces.

SHELL SOAP

Shell Soap presented the collection's most complex geometric challenge. Based on logarithmic spirals found in gastropod shells, it required multiple prototyping iterations to achieve the correct coil ratio. The spiral creates natural drainage channels and offers multiple grip orientations. Each color variant pairs with complementary scents — pink with rose, blue with ocean — building a connection between visual and olfactory experiences. The decreasing radius ensures ergonomic properties remain consistent as the soap diminishes.

STAR SOAP

Star Soap explores form and function through five-pointed geometry enhanced with phosphorescent material. The glow-in-the-dark element adds an interactive quality, while the vertices provide natural grip points. Its rotational symmetry means there's no wrong way to hold it, addressing common frustrations with directional soap designs.

GRIP SOAP

Grip Soap represents the most technically driven design in the collection. Its faceted geometry features precisely angled ridges optimized for wet-hand friction. The embossed Slug logo on the front serves triple duty: brand identification, added grip texture, and visual orientation indicator. The charcoal variant incorporates remnants from other soaps, creating a zero-waste option with unique marbled patterns while maintaining identical functional properties.

BRAND IDENTITY

Slug Soap's visual identity draws inspiration from pixelated graphics and cross-stitch patterns, blending digital and handmade aesthetics. This approach runs across the brand's website, packaging, and merchandise to create a cohesive system that adapts across mediums.

Type

Slug Soap uses Monument Grotesk Mono and Eucrosia UPC Italic to create contrast in brand communications. Monument, a monospaced sans-serif, handles informational text like product descriptions and website content, prioritizing readability with its uniform spacing and clear letterforms. Eucrosia UPC Italic adds character through its fluid strokes, used for headers and emphasis to create visual interest against Monument's structured forms.

Logo Design

The Slug Soap logo centers on a pixelated snail mark with a distinct spiral shell, reflecting the brand's digital-meets-handmade aesthetic. While the name references the experimental phase of soap making (when its slippery texture reminded me of slug trails), the snail became a more approachable character for the visual identity. This intentional contrast adds intrigue to the brand story. The pixelated spiral has become a recognizable element that ties the visual system together.

PACKAGING

For the Star, Grip, Flower, and Shell soaps, I designed packaging that captures the brand's personality while enhancing the overall product experience. My goal was to create a versatile container accommodating most soap shapes in a cohesive, memorable way.

Each soap comes in a minimal off-white box featuring an embossed snail logo on the front and 'slug' on the bottom, creating a tactile experience that mirrors the textural quality of the soap itself and engages users before they even see the product.

Opening the box reveals an energetic interior featuring Slug Soap's full cast of characters rendered in two-tone dark and light green. This stark contrast between the understated exterior and dynamic interior creates an intentional reveal during unboxing.

The packaging uses 18pt 100% post-consumer waste paperboard, chosen for its embossing capability and environmental sustainability. The natural off-white tone of the recycled stock adds subtle depth to the exterior embossing, while the interior features custom green soy-based inks in two tones for the illustrated elements. The box uses a tabbed lock-bottom construction for stability and clean edges, ensuring structural integrity and a premium unboxing experience.

MERCH

Beyond soap, I developed a merchandise line that extends Slug into daily life. This collection includes screen-printed t-shirts with the pixelated snail logo, stainless steel water bottles with etched designs, and vinyl sticker sheets featuring the full cast of Slug characters. Each piece maintains the brand's visual language while serving as functional items customers can integrate into their routines, creating additional touchpoints for engagement beyond the bathroom.

PRODUCTION PROCESS

Each design required custom molds developed using 3D printing and silicone casting techniques. Production involves precise temperature control and timing to ensure consistent results. I adjust material formulations by design — the Star Soap requires increased hardness for point retention, while Shell Soap needs flexibility for curved surfaces. This iterative process of prototyping, testing, and refinement continues to inform new variations and improvements.

For Slug Soap's e-commerce platform, I focused on creating a streamlined interface that prioritizes product discovery while maintaining essential brand elements. The site architecture reflects the same balance of simplicity and surprise found in the physical products.

Information Architecture

The site employs a split-screen layout with scrollable product catalog on the left and fixed navigation on the right. This approach keeps products front and center while ensuring key functions remain accessible. Each product card follows a consistent template: hero image, detailed description, and prominent 'Add to Cart' button, separated by subtle dividers for visual clarity. The fixed sidebar contains company information, navigation links, and social media connections, creating a persistent brand presence without overwhelming the shopping experience.

Responsive Strategy

On mobile devices, the layout adapts through a fundamental restructuring rather than simple scaling. The fixed sidebar elements migrate to a sticky header, maintaining functional parity while optimizing for touch interactions. Product cards expand to full width with larger tap targets, and the cart interface slides in from the right to preserve screen real estate. This responsive approach ensures consistent functionality across devices while acknowledging the different interaction patterns of desktop and mobile users.

Visual Design

The interface extends the brand's pixel aesthetic through subtle grid alignments and monospaced typography. Product photography maintains consistent lighting and background treatment to create visual cohesion within the catalog. Hover states on desktop reveal additional product angles, while mobile users can swipe through images. The minimal color palette allows product colors to dominate, with interface elements using only neutral tones and the brand's signature green for interactive elements.

Technical Implementation

Built on a lightweight framework prioritizing performance, the site loads quickly despite image-heavy content. Lazy loading prevents unnecessary bandwidth usage, while optimized images maintain quality at minimal file sizes. The cart system uses session storage to preserve selections without requiring accounts, reducing friction in the purchase process. Payment integration through Stripe enables secure transactions while maintaining the streamlined aesthetic.

I've created a range of motion projects spanning new soap releases, event promotions, and brand identity content. Using Blender, I built 3D product visualizations that highlighted each soap's form, such as the precise spiral of the Shell Soap and the geometry of the Star Soap, creating a cohesive catalog of rotating renders that emphasized their material and sculptural qualities. In After Effects, I made animated versions of the pixelated snail logo and supporting graphics with a visual style referencing early digital graphics, reinforcing Slug's digital-meets-handcrafted identity.
